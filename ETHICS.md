# Ethics & Source Disclosure

## Source Material
The principles documented in this project were derived from an architectural analysis of publicly available, leaked AI system prompts (specifically, those associated with advanced frontier models).

**Important Note:** No verbatim prompt text, proprietary formatting, or confidential instructions are reproduced in this repository. The project only extracts, abstracts, and discusses the generalized interaction patterns and behavioral principles observed in the source material.

## Independence
This project is an independent, community-driven effort. It is **not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to** any AI company, including the creators of the original source prompts. The names of specific models or companies may be referenced strictly for academic and analytical context.

## Fair Use Position
We believe the analysis provided here constitutes transformative fair use under copyright law. The purpose of this project is commentary, criticism, and education. By abstracting specific prompt instructions into generalized design patterns, this project aims to advance the open-source community's understanding of safe, effective, and humane AI interaction design.

## AI Safety Responsibility
Some principles discussed in this repository—particularly those relating to boundary setting and detection mechanics—are designed to make safety refusals feel less robotic and more humane.

**Misuse Warning:** These principles must not be weaponized to build deceptive AI systems or to intentionally obscure safety mechanisms from appropriate oversight. While the principles advocate for communicating safety boundaries with dignity to the end-user, the underlying safety mechanisms must remain fully auditable, explainable, and transparent to system operators, safety researchers, and regulators. Misusing these principles to build unaccountable systems is contrary to the goals of this project.
